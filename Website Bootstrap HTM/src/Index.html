Mr. Devid Smith

Roland David Smith was an American artist who was associated with the Abstract Expressionist movement and is known for his large-scale geometric sculptures made of steel. He was born on March 9, 1906, in Decatur, Indiana, and grew up in a family of farmers. Smith attended Ohio University and later moved to New York City to pursue his artistic career. He studied at the Art Students League and became interested in the work of Pablo Picasso and the Surrealist movement.

Later on, he began to experiment with sculpture and developed his own unique style, which combined abstraction with industrial materials such as steel. His sculptures were often massive, with angular forms and a sense of tension and movement. In addition to sculpture, Smith was also a painter and printmaker. He died on December 23, 1965, in Vermont, leaving behind a legacy as one of the most important sculptors of the 20th century.
